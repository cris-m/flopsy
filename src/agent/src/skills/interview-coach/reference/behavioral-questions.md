# Behavioral Interview Questions

Organized by competency. Use with the STAR framework (Situation → Task → Action → Result).

## Leadership & Ownership

1. Tell me about a time you led a project from start to finish.
2. Describe a situation where you had to make a decision without all the information.
3. Tell me about a time you took ownership of something outside your job description.
4. Describe a time you had to convince your team to change direction.
5. Tell me about a time you identified a problem before anyone else did.
6. Give an example of when you had to lead without formal authority.
7. Describe a situation where you had to prioritize multiple competing deadlines.
8. Tell me about a time you mentored or coached someone.

## Conflict & Disagreement

9. Tell me about a time you disagreed with your manager. How did you handle it?
10. Describe a conflict with a coworker and how you resolved it.
11. Tell me about a time you received feedback you disagreed with.
12. Describe a situation where two stakeholders wanted opposite things.
13. Tell me about a time you had to push back on a request.
14. Describe a time when a team member was not pulling their weight.

## Failure & Adversity

15. Tell me about your biggest professional failure. What did you learn?
16. Describe a time a project you led failed or went off track.
17. Tell me about a time you made a mistake that affected the team.
18. Describe a situation where you were under extreme pressure.
19. Tell me about a time you missed a deadline. What happened?
20. Describe a time when you had to deal with ambiguity and uncertainty.

## Teamwork & Collaboration

21. Tell me about a time you worked with a difficult team member.
22. Describe a successful cross-functional collaboration.
23. Tell me about a time you had to rely on others to complete your work.
24. Describe a time you helped a struggling teammate.
25. Tell me about a project that required coordinating across multiple teams.
26. Describe a time you had to adapt your communication style for a different audience.

## Growth & Learning

27. Tell me about a skill you taught yourself recently.
28. Describe a time you had to learn something quickly for a project.
29. Tell me about the most useful feedback you have ever received.
30. Describe a time you changed your approach based on new information.
31. Tell me about a time you stepped outside your comfort zone.
32. What is the most important lesson you learned in the last year?

## Customer & Impact Focus

33. Tell me about a time you went above and beyond for a customer or user.
34. Describe a time you used data to make a decision.
35. Tell me about a time you simplified something complex.
36. Describe a situation where you had to balance speed with quality.
37. Tell me about a time you delivered a project that had measurable impact.
38. Describe a time you had to say no to a customer or stakeholder.

## Innovation & Problem Solving

39. Tell me about a time you came up with a creative solution to a problem.
40. Describe a time you improved an existing process.
41. Tell me about a time you built something from scratch.
42. Describe a situation where the obvious solution was wrong.
43. Tell me about a time you had to work with limited resources.

## STAR Framework Reminder

For every answer:
- **Situation**: Set the scene (1-2 sentences, be specific: team size, timeline, stakes)
- **Task**: What was YOUR responsibility? (not the team's — yours)
- **Action**: What did YOU specifically do? (concrete steps, decisions, tools)
- **Result**: What happened? (quantify: numbers, percentages, outcomes, lessons learned)

### Common Pitfalls
- Using "we" too much — interviewers want to know what YOU did
- Skipping the result — always end with the outcome
- Being too vague — "it went well" is not a result
- Choosing the wrong example — pick stories with clear, positive outcomes
- Going too long — aim for 1.5-2 minutes per answer
