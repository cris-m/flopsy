# Case Study & Estimation Questions

For PM, consulting, business analyst, and general problem-solving interviews.

## Market Sizing / Estimation

1. How many piano tuners are there in Chicago?
2. Estimate the number of gas stations in the United States.
3. How much revenue does a single McDonald's location generate per year?
4. Estimate the total storage needed for all YouTube videos.
5. How many tennis balls fit in this room?
6. Estimate the number of queries Google processes per second.
7. How much does it cost to run Uber in New York City for a day?

### How to Approach Estimation
1. **Clarify** — what are we estimating exactly? Define the scope.
2. **Break down** — decompose into smaller, estimable pieces.
3. **Anchor** — start with numbers you know (population, averages, percentages).
4. **Calculate** — walk through the math out loud.
5. **Sanity check** — does the final number feel reasonable?

### Example: "How many piano tuners in Chicago?"
- Chicago population: ~2.7M people
- Average household size: ~2.5 → ~1.1M households
- % of households with a piano: ~5% → 55,000 pianos
- Tuning frequency: ~1-2 per year → ~80,000 tunings/year
- A tuner does ~4 tunings/day, 250 working days/year → 1,000 tunings/year per tuner
- 80,000 / 1,000 = **~80 piano tuners**

The exact number does not matter — the approach and reasoning do.

## Product / Strategy Cases

8. Should Spotify launch a hardware device? Why or why not?
9. How would you improve Instagram's Explore page?
10. A ride-sharing app is seeing declining rides in a major city. Diagnose and propose solutions.
11. You are the PM for a new feature at Amazon. How do you decide what to build next?
12. A subscription product has high sign-up rates but low retention after month 3. What do you investigate?
13. How would you design a loyalty program for a coffee chain?
14. Should a bank build its own mobile app or partner with a fintech?

### Framework: Product Cases
1. **Clarify** — what is the goal? Who is the user? What constraints?
2. **Structure** — break into user segments, use cases, or decision criteria
3. **Analyze** — evaluate options using data, trade-offs, and user impact
4. **Recommend** — pick a direction with clear reasoning
5. **Measure** — how would you know if it worked? Define success metrics.

## Operations / Process Cases

15. A warehouse is experiencing a 15% error rate in order fulfillment. How do you fix it?
16. Customer support tickets have increased 40% this quarter. What do you do?
17. A team of 8 engineers is shipping features slowly. Diagnose and improve.
18. Your SaaS product has 99.5% uptime but the SLA promises 99.9%. Action plan?
19. A data pipeline that runs nightly is increasingly failing. How do you stabilize it?

### Framework: Operations Cases
1. **Scope the problem** — quantify it, understand the timeline, identify who is affected
2. **Hypothesize causes** — list 3-5 most likely root causes
3. **Investigate** — what data would you look at? What questions would you ask?
4. **Propose solutions** — short-term fix + long-term fix, with trade-offs
5. **Implement and measure** — rollout plan, success metrics, monitoring

## Ethical / Judgment Cases

20. You discover your product is being used to spread misinformation. What do you do?
21. A major customer is threatening to leave unless you build their custom feature. How do you handle it?
22. Your team's top performer is toxic to junior engineers. What do you do?
23. You are asked to ship a feature you believe is bad for users but good for revenue. How do you respond?
24. A competitor just launched your planned feature. What is your strategy?

## Evaluation Rubric for Case Answers

| Dimension | Strong | Needs Work |
|-----------|--------|------------|
| **Structure** | Clear framework, logical decomposition | Stream of consciousness, no organization |
| **Assumptions** | States assumptions explicitly, checks if reasonable | Makes hidden assumptions, does not validate |
| **Quantitative reasoning** | Uses numbers, estimates, does math out loud | All qualitative, no numbers, hand-wavy |
| **Creativity** | Considers non-obvious angles, second-order effects | Only surface-level, obvious answers |
| **Communication** | Thinks out loud, checks in with interviewer | Silent for long periods, does not explain reasoning |
| **Conclusion** | Clear recommendation with supporting logic | Ends without a recommendation, or hedges everything |
